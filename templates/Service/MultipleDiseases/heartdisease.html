Understanding the Parameters:
  • Age: Your age is an important factor in assessing heart disease risk.
  • Sex: Gender plays a role in heart disease risk, with men generally at higher risk.
  • Chest Pain Type: Different types of chest pain can indicate different levels of heart disease risk.
  • Resting Blood Pressure: High blood pressure can increase the risk of heart disease.
  • Serum Cholesterol: High cholesterol levels are linked to heart disease.
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: High blood sugar can be a sign of diabetes, which increases heart disease risk.
  • Resting ECG Results: An electrocardiogram measures your heart's electrical activity and can show abnormalities.
  • Maximum Heart Rate Achieved: How fast your heart beats during exercise can indicate heart health.
  • Exercise Induced Angina: Chest pain during exercise can be a sign of heart disease.
  • ST Depression: A measure of how much your heart's electrical activity changes during stress.
  • Slope of the Peak Exercise ST Segment: Indicates the shape of the heart's response to exercise.
  • Number of Major Vessels: The number of blood vessels with significant narrowing.
  • Thalium Stress Result: A type of test that shows how well blood flows into the heart during activity.
