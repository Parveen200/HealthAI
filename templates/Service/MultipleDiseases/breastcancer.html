Parameter Information
  • Clump Thickness: Measures the thickness of the clumps of cells. A higher value indicates a thicker clump.
  • Uniform Cell Size: Indicates how uniform the size of the cells are. A higher value means more uniform cells.
  • Uniform Cell Shape: Assesses the shape of cells. A higher value means cells have a more uniform shape.
  • Marginal Adhesion: Measures how well the cells stick together at the margins. Higher values suggest better adhesion.
  • Single Epithelial Cell Size: Size of individual epithelial cells. Higher values indicate larger cells.
  • Bare Nuclei: Counts the number of nuclei that are not surrounded by a cell. Higher values indicate more bare nuclei.
  • Bland Chromatin: Evaluates the texture of the chromatin. Higher values denote a more bland or uniform chromatin texture.
  • Normal Nucleoli: Measures the presence of nucleoli in cells. Higher values indicate more normal nucleoli.
  • Mitoses: Indicates the number of mitotic cells. Higher values show a higher rate of cell division.
